M2 Module 2 — Subsidiary Books, Cash Book & Bank Reconciliation Statement HIGH
💡 Exam Slots: Q3 = Cash Book practical (always Triple Column). Q4 = BRS (always practical + definition). Both appear every single year.
⚠️ Common Mistakes in Cash Book: (1) Forgetting contra entries — when cash deposited to bank, both Cash cr and Bank dr columns are filled (C mark in folio). (2) Mixing up discount columns — discount allowed is Dr side, discount received is Cr side.
⚠️ Common Mistakes in BRS: (1) Wrong direction of adjustment — unpresented cheques ADD to bank balance, uncleared deposits LESS from bank balance. (2) Confusing Cash Book balance with Pass Book balance as starting point.

M3 Module 3 — Final Accounts: Trading Account, P&L Account, Balance Sheet HIGH
💡 Exam Slot: Q7 every year — always 12–15 marks. Theory (2–3m) + Full Accounts from Trial Balance with 3–4 adjustments (12m). This is the highest single mark question.
⚠️ Critical Adjustments you MUST know: (1) Closing Stock — goes to Trading A/c credit side AND Balance Sheet asset side. (2) Prepaid Expenses — reduce expense, show in BS as current asset. (3) Outstanding Expenses — add to expense, show in BS as current liability. (4) Depreciation — reduce asset, charge to P&L debit. Always check: if adjustment is "given in Trial Balance" it goes only once; if given "outside TB" it goes twice.

M4 Module 4 — Capital/Revenue Expenditure, Bad Debts, Provisions, Adjustments HIGH
💡 Exam Slot: Q6 every year — always theory-based. Either short notes (2×4=8) or distinguish (5+7=12). This is a pure theory question — no practicals.

Key PYQ Questions

M4 4/4 years 15 marks

M4 4/4 years 7 marks
2025 Q6 (a) Short note: Bad Debts, Provision for Doubtful Debts (5m). (b) Distinguish Capital vs Revenue Expenditure (10m).
M4 4/4 years 15 marks

Key Points to Prepare

  • Capital Expenditure: Increases earning capacity, long-term benefit, appears in Balance Sheet, e.g. purchase of machinery, building
  • Revenue Expenditure: Maintains earning capacity, short-term, charged to P&L, e.g. salaries, repairs, rent
  • Bad Debts: Debts confirmed to be irrecoverable; written off directly to P&L A/c
  • Provision for Doubtful Debts: Created against debts that MIGHT not be recoverable; shown as deduction from debtors in BS
  • Outstanding Expenses: Incurred but not yet paid; current liability in BS
  • Prepaid Expenses: Paid but benefit not yet received; current asset in BS
✅ Quick Win: Capital vs Revenue is asked every year — prepare a comparison table of 6–8 points with examples. This alone secures 8–10 marks.
M5 Module 5 — Depreciation Accounting HIGH
💡 Exam Slot: Q5 every year — split into theory sub-parts. Always asked: meaning + causes + methods. 2023 & 2024 also added: factors in calculating + mandatory under Companies Act.

Key PYQ Questions

2022 Q5 (a) Explain meaning of Depreciation. Write the methods of Depreciation (3+5=8). (b) Write the causes of Depreciation (7).
M5 4/4 years 15 marks
2023 Q4 What is depreciation? What factors are considered in calculating depreciation? What is the main purpose of writing off depreciation? Is depreciation mandatory as per Companies Act 1956? (4+4+4+3=15).
M5 2/4 years 15 marks
2025 Q5 (a) What is Depreciation? Write the method of calculating Depreciation (3+5=8). (b) Write the causes of Depreciation (7).
M5 4/4 years 15 marks

Key Points to Prepare

  • Meaning: Gradual decrease in value of fixed assets due to use, wear and tear, time, obsolescence
  • Causes: Wear & tear, Efflux of time, Obsolescence, Accidents, Depletion, Permanent fall in market price
  • Methods: (1) Straight Line Method (SLM) — fixed amount each year. (2) Written Down Value (WDV) / Diminishing Balance — fixed % on reducing balance. (3) Sum of Years Digits. (4) Units of Production. (5) Depletion method.
  • Factors: Cost of asset, Estimated useful life, Residual/Scrap value, Obsolescence rate
  • Purpose: Correctly show asset value, compute profit, replace asset when worn out, comply with law
✅ Key Insight: Q5 of 2022 and 2025 are nearly identical. Prepare SLM vs WDV comparison table (5–6 points) + causes list (6 points) and you have Q5 fully covered.

M2 Cash Book & Bank Reconciliation Statement ⭐ HIGHEST PRIORITY
⚠️ M2 carries the highest marks density. Triple Column Cash Book appeared all 4 years (2022–2025) for 10–12 marks each. BRS appeared all 4 years for 8–12 marks. Together these two practicals alone = ~22–24 marks (31% of total marks 70). You cannot pass without mastering these.
TopicFreqStatusWhat exactly to study / what to skip
Triple Column Cash Book — Preparation 4/4 ✅ MUST Format: Dr side (Cash | Discount | Bank), Cr side (Cash | Discount | Bank). Key rules: contra entry marked 'C', discount is memo column (never balanced), personal withdrawals = Drawings. Practise 2022, 2024, 2025 problems (nearly identical). 2023 has endorsement quirk.
Bank Reconciliation Statement — Preparation 4/4 ✅ MUST Start from Cash Book balance. Add: unpresented cheques. Deduct: uncleared deposits. Add: direct bank credits. Deduct: bank charges, OD interest. 2025 Q4(b) ≡ 2022 Q4(b) — same structure, different numbers. Master the adjustment table format.
Types of Cash Book 4/4 ✅ MUST 4 types: (1) Simple/Single Column, (2) Double Column (cash+discount), (3) Triple Column (cash+bank+discount), (4) Petty Cash Book. Know definition + key feature of each. Asked as 2–5 mark theory every year.
Causes of difference — Cash Book vs Pass Book 4/4 ✅ MUST 4 main causes: (1) Cheques issued not presented, (2) Cheques deposited not cleared, (3) Bank charges/interest not recorded in CB, (4) Direct debits/credits by bank. Write as numbered list with example for each.
Definition & advantages of BRS 3/4 MUST Definition: statement to reconcile CB and PB balance. Advantages: detects errors, prevents fraud, confirms actual bank balance, improves internal control. 3–4 marks easy scoring.
Definition & advantages of Cash Book 2/4 STUDY Advantages: saves time, acts as both journal and ledger, shows cash position daily, prevents fraud. 2–3 mark question.
Subsidiary Books — other types (Purchase book, Sales book etc.) 0/4 SKIP Mentioned in syllabus but never asked in 4 years. At most know names: Purchase Book, Sales Book, Purchase Returns, Sales Returns, Bills Books. No preparation needed.
✅ M2 Study Plan: Triple Column Cash Book practice (60 min, do all 4 PYQ problems) → BRS preparation practice (45 min, do all 4 PYQ problems) → Types of CB theory (15 min) → Causes of difference (10 min) → BRS definition/advantages (10 min). Total: ~2.5 hrs
M1 Concept of Accounts, Journal, Ledger, Trial Balance ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
⚠️ M1 is the foundation of all objectives (10 marks Q1) and Journal practicals (10–12 marks Q2). Journal entries appeared 4/4 years as the Q2(b) for 10–12 marks. Golden rules are tested every single year. Objectives heavily draw from M1 concepts.
TopicFreqStatusWhat exactly to study / what to skip
Journal Entries — Practical Problems 4/4 ✅ MUST Know golden rules by heart. Common entries: started business (Cash Dr/Capital Cr), goods purchased (Purchase Dr/Cash or Creditor Cr), goods sold (Cash or Debtor Dr/Sales Cr), discount allowed/received, contra entries, depreciation. Practise all 4 PYQ sets.
Golden Rules of Debit and Credit 4/4 ✅ MUST 3 rules verbatim: Personal A/c — Dr the receiver, Cr the giver. Real A/c — Dr what comes in, Cr what goes out. Nominal A/c — Dr all expenses & losses, Cr all incomes & gains. Asked as 2–5 mark theory every year.
Definition of Journal / Account 3/4 MUST Journal: book of original/primary entry, records transactions chronologically with narration. Account: complete record of transactions related to a particular person or item. Know 2–3 line definition each.
Distinguish: Journal vs Ledger 3/4 MUST Table of 6–8 differences. Key points: Journal = primary entry, chronological, narration required. Ledger = secondary entry, account-wise, narration not required, shows final position. 2023 had this as 8-mark question.
Classification of Accounts 3/4 MUST 3 classes: Personal (Natural persons, Artificial — companies, Representative — outstanding expenses), Real (Tangible — cash, goods; Intangible — goodwill), Nominal (income, expenses). 2025 asked "types of account" for 5 marks.
Double Entry System 2/4 STUDY Every transaction has two equal and opposite effects. Advantages: complete record, detects errors, helps prepare final accounts. 2024 Q2(a) asked for 2 marks — brief definition sufficient.
Ledger — posting rules, balancing 1/4 STUDY Rules: post from journal, opposite side, write "To" and "By". Balance = difference between Dr and Cr totals. Rarely asked as standalone — more useful for practicals.
Trial Balance — definition, objectives, preparation 1/4 STUDY List of all ledger balances; proves arithmetical accuracy. Debit balances: assets, expenses, drawings, losses. Credit balances: liabilities, capital, income. Appeared in 2024 Q1(f) as fill-in — know definition.
Advantages of Journal 1/4 SKIM 5 advantages: chronological record, minimizes error, narration explains transaction, facilitates posting, prevents fraud. Just memorize bullet list.
✅ M1 Study Plan: Golden Rules (20 min memorize) → Journal entries practical — all 4 PYQ sets (60 min) → Classification of Accounts (20 min) → Journal vs Ledger distinctions (20 min) → Double Entry definition (10 min). Total: ~2.5 hrs
M3 Final Accounts — Trading, P&L, Balance Sheet ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
⚠️ Q7 is reserved entirely for Final Accounts every single year. The 12–15 mark practical appeared 4/4 years. The 2022 and 2025 Trial Balances are literally identical (same figures, same adjustments). Meaning of Trading Account also asked 4/4 years as a 3-mark opener. This module alone = ~15–18 marks.
TopicFreqStatusWhat exactly to study / what to skip
Full Final Accounts from Trial Balance (with adjustments) 4/4 ✅ MUST Sequence: (1) Trading A/c → Gross Profit. (2) P&L A/c → Net Profit. (3) Balance Sheet. Adjustments always tested: Closing Stock, Prepaid expenses, Outstanding expenses, Depreciation. Practise 2022=2025 (identical), 2023, 2024 problems.
Meaning of Trading Account 4/4 ✅ MUST Definition (3 marks): Part of Final Accounts that shows Gross Profit/Loss. Dr side: Opening Stock, Purchases (less returns), Direct expenses (carriage in, wages). Cr side: Sales (less returns), Closing Stock. Gross Profit = Cr side − Dr side.
Meaning of Balance Sheet 2/4 STUDY Statement showing Assets, Liabilities and Capital on a specific date. Not an account — no Dr/Cr. Shows financial position. Both sides must tally. 2–3 marks.
Preparation of P&L Account 4/4 MUST Dr side: Gross Loss b/d, all indirect expenses (salaries, rent, depreciation, bad debts, provision). Cr side: Gross Profit b/d, all incomes. Net Profit transferred to Capital in B/S.
Why Balance Sheet at year-end 1/4 SKIM 2023 Q7(a) — 2 marks. Brief answer: financial year-end ensures all transactions recorded; provides year-end comparison; statutory requirement; all accounts closed for the period.
✅ M3 Study Plan: Meaning of Trading A/c (15 min) → Practise 2022/2025 Trial Balance problem — identical, master once (45 min) → Practise 2023 problem (30 min) → Practise 2024 problem (30 min) → Balance Sheet meaning (10 min). Total: ~2 hrs
M4 Capital & Revenue Expenditure, Bad Debts, Provisions, Adjustments 📚 STUDY
⚠️ Q6 is reserved for M4 theory every year. Capital vs Revenue Expenditure distinguished 4/4 years (2025 asked 10 marks — most detailed). Bad Debts / Provision asked 4/4 years. Both are easy theory questions with high marks payoff.
TopicFreqStatusWhat exactly to study / what to skip
Capital Expenditure vs Revenue Expenditure 4/4 ✅ MUST Table format with 8–10 points: Nature (long-term vs short-term), Effect (creates asset vs maintains), Frequency (non-recurring vs recurring), Benefit period, Examples (machine/building vs salary/rent/repairs), Treatment (Balance Sheet vs P&L). 2025 asks 10 marks — prepare full table.
Bad Debts — meaning & treatment 4/4 ✅ MUST Meaning: irrecoverable debt, actual loss. Journal: Bad Debts A/c Dr / Debtors Cr. Then: P&L A/c Dr / Bad Debts Cr. In Final A/c: deducted from Debtors in B/S, shown as expense in P&L. 2–5 marks every year.
Provision for Bad and Doubtful Debts 4/4 ✅ MUST Precautionary measure, not actual loss. Journal: P&L A/c Dr / Provision for Doubtful Debts Cr. In B/S: deducted from Debtors. Distinction from Bad Debts: Provision = anticipated, Bad Debt = confirmed. 3–4 marks.
Distinguish Bad Debts vs Doubtful Debts 2/4 STUDY 2022 Q6(b) asked 5 marks on this distinction. 5 points: certainty, treatment, timing, reversibility, impact on debtors. If you know both concepts separately, the distinction writes itself.
Outstanding Expenses (concept) 1/4 SKIM Expense incurred but not yet paid. Add to expense in P&L; show as current liability in B/S. Mostly tested as adjustment within Q7, not standalone. Wages outstanding (2022, 2025 Q7) = classic example.
Prepaid Expenses (concept) 1/4 SKIM Paid in advance for next period. Deduct from expense in P&L; show as current asset in B/S. Rent prepaid appears in 2023, 2024, 2025 Q7 adjustments.
Accrued Income (concept) 0/4 SKIP Income earned but not received. Never asked as standalone in 4 years. Only know it exists. Zero exam risk.
Provision for Discount on Debtors 0/4 SKIP Never asked in 4 years — not even in objectives. Zero exam risk. Skip entirely.
✅ M4 Study Plan: Capital vs Revenue distinction — write 8-point table (25 min) → Bad Debts meaning + journal entry (15 min) → Provision for Doubtful Debts (15 min) → Prepaid/Outstanding (10 min). Total: ~65 min
M5 Depreciation Accounting 📚 STUDY
⚠️ Q5 is reserved for Depreciation every year. Definition + methods appeared 4/4 years. Causes appeared in 2022 and 2025 for 7 marks each. 2023 and 2024 had "factors + purpose + mandatory?" combination. Total marks from M5 ≈ 15 marks every year. All theory — no calculations asked yet.
TopicFreqStatusWhat exactly to study / what to skip
Meaning / Definition of Depreciation 4/4 ✅ MUST Permanent and continuous decrease in the value of a fixed asset due to use, passage of time, or obsolescence. Distinguish from Fluctuation (temporary). Know 2–3 different author definitions.
Causes of Depreciation 3/4 ✅ MUST 7 causes: (1) Physical wear & tear, (2) Passage of time, (3) Obsolescence (new technology), (4) Depletion (mines, quarries), (5) Inadequacy (asset too small for business), (6) Accidents, (7) Legal restrictions/expiry of lease. Write all 7 with brief explanation.
Methods of Depreciation 4/4 ✅ MUST Must know 2 main methods: (1) SLM (Fixed Installment / Straight Line): fixed amount = (Cost−Scrap)/Life. (2) WDV (Diminishing Balance / Reducing): fixed % on reducing balance. Also know: Revaluation, Annuity, Sinking Fund, Machine Hour Rate (name only). Compare SLM vs WDV for 5+ marks.
Factors in Calculating Depreciation 2/4 STUDY 4 factors: (1) Original cost of asset, (2) Estimated useful life, (3) Residual/Scrap value, (4) Method selected. 2023 and 2024 Q5(a) both asked this for 4 marks.
Purpose of Depreciation / Why Charge Depreciation 2/4 STUDY 4 purposes: (1) True profit calculation, (2) Correct asset valuation, (3) Replacement fund creation, (4) Tax benefit. 2023 Q4(b) asked specifically — 4 marks.
Depreciation under Companies Act / Income Tax Act 2/4 STUDY Companies Act: mandatory under Schedule II (useful life basis). Income Tax Act: mandatory, WDV method prescribed, specific rates for each asset class. 2023 Q4(b), 2024 Q5(b) asked this. 4–7 marks.
Numerical depreciation calculations (SLM/WDV ledger) 0/4 SKIP Never asked as a standalone depreciation calculation in 4 years. Depreciation appears only as an adjustment in Q7 (Final Accounts). No need to practise depreciation ledger accounts separately.
✅ M5 Study Plan: Definition of Depreciation (10 min) → Causes — all 7 (20 min) → SLM vs WDV comparison table (25 min) → Factors in calculation (10 min) → Purpose of Depreciation (10 min) → Acts (15 min). Total: ~90 min
